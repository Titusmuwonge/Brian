About Us

American Education and Teachers is more than an organization — it’s a movement of compassion, empowerment, and transformation in Uganda.

Our Story

Founded in 2000 in the village of Kaihura, Uganda, American Education and Teachers was born out of a deep commitment to restore dignity and create opportunities for children and families affected by poverty, disease, and displacement.

What started as a small effort to care for orphaned children has grown into a vibrant community-driven initiative serving thousands through education, healthcare, vocational training, and sustainable development programs.

Mission & Vision

Our Mission

To restore hope and improve the quality of life for orphans, vulnerable children, and families in Uganda by providing education, healthcare, shelter, and pathways to self-sustainability.

Our Vision

A thriving community where every child has the chance to grow in dignity, families are self-sustaining, and future generations rise free from cycles of poverty and hopelessness.

Our Leadership

American Education and Teachers is guided by dedicated leaders in both Uganda and the USA. Together, they ensure accountability, transparency, and long-term sustainability in every initiative.

Their vision is rooted in faith, compassion, and a firm belief that communities can rise when given the right tools, resources, and opportunities.
