Our Mission

American Education and Teachers is the sister organization of Bringing Hope to the Family Uganda established in 2000. Together, we are a nonprofit organization which continues to provide basic needs, education, and clean water to orphans and displaced families in the Kaihura Village of Kyenjojo District, Uganda.

Our Story

Our Programs

We provide comprehensive support to orphans and displaced families through education, medical care, emergency relief, and sustainable farming.

Education

New Hope Vocational Training Institute provides skills in tailoring, knitting, carpentry, and more for orphans who were unable to return to school.

Hope Again Nursery and Primary School specializes in providing education and care to children with disabilities.

Learn More

Medical

Hope Again Medical Clinic offers HIV and AIDS positive treatment to orphans and their caretakers, in addition to general medical services.

Hope Again Children's Home cares for children ages 2 months to 17 years old by providing opportunities for education and healthy lives.

Learn More

Emergency Relief Fund

We have created an emergency relief fund to address unexpected specific medical needs such as discovery of cancers, tumors, chronic illnesses, and congenital diseases.

Oftentimes, it's orphans who are in need of emergency medical procedures who benefit from this relief fund.

Learn More

Scholarships

We serve our families and children for more than just a particular season. We fund as many young adults as financially possible into higher education.

Our goal is for all BHTF children to have the opportunity of seeking higher education.

Learn More

Sustainability

We walk alongside BHTF's children and families throughout their lives because we believe the key to changing the future of Uganda is fostering long-term sustainability.

Kyongera Farm provides access to healthy produce for students, families, and staff of BHTF.
